PADI Instructor, world-traveler, and creator Steve Belkin was in downtown Cozumel and intrigued by the cement structures found underwater directly off the shore from Hemmingway Restaurant.
Finding the location was the easy part, especially since the boat ride to the site is only 2 minutes. For Steve, the hardest part was developing something that was just the right amount of challenging: not too easy but not too hard.
“This is nothing like a drift dive inside the national marine park, and we're not a traditional dive shop. We don't teach courses. We are an activity center. Consider this an added activity to what you have already set up with your regular dive center.”
If there is a word to describe Steve Belkin, work history, it would be unpredictable. Steve has veered and zigzagged through thirty years of absolutely disconnected experiences.
With Scuba Diving, he went from zero to hero, from Open Water to an instructor in less than 4 months and he has logged over 1100 dives in less than 3 years, including 250 during the peak of COVID. No part of the world has been safe from Steve's scuba invasion even some remote places in the Philippines, Thailand, India, and Mozambique.
Island resident, Steve Belkin, is not only the creator of Scuba Hunt, but he's also the author of “Mileage Maniac: My Genius, Madness and a Touch of Evil, to Amass 40 Million Frequent Flyer Miles,” available on Amazon.
